The Graduate School Social Media

Introduction

Graduate school is usually an intermediatery step in a person's career. The experience is to build skills and a network to eventually pursue an academic endeavor or industry role. As The Graduate School at the Unviversity of Connecticut, our team supported graduate students along their academic journeys through resources, programming, events, conflict mediation and other services.

My role as a Communications Designer was to create visual assets and copy for various social media, email, and web experiences. This particular case study will focus on my work in social media.

Overview

Roles: Communications Designer, Social Media Strategist

Team: Marie Leblanc (Communications Lead), Jia Cai (Programming Assistant)

Duration: 12 Weeks (and ongoing project)

Results

Sharing Resources and Events

Our mission at The Graduate School is to help students along their journey. At different points in the semester, we created guides and shared resources to help students in their academic journey. This was particularly important for remote students, where resources were most likely to be seen online or on social media.

Keeping Students on Track with Reminders for Major Deadlines

Especially for students, it's easy for important deadlines to creep up quickly. To make sure graduate students didn't miss a thing, we shared the biggest academic ones that could reach them through their social feed.

Spotlighting UConn Students and Alumni

There are some amazing students and alumni from the University of Connecticut. We wanted to highlight their accomplishments to show current students their Husky community as well as to give prospective students an idea of what they can achieve.

Promoting Other Departmental Content

If it takes a village to raise a child, it takes a community of departments to raise a graduate student. There were many events and resources that were shared by the main UConn brand and sibling departments. It was important to use to share this content and increase its reach.

Celebrating National, International, and Cultural Holidays

We commemorated the events and holidays that fall outside of academia. Our strategy was to celebrate common holidays alongside ones that promoted kindness and self-care. The latter was particularly important for stressful times such as mideterm and finals weeks.

Campus Photos to Highlight UConn

A fun campaign we started was to post seasonal pictures of on-campus scenery. We hoped to showcase the wonderful sights at UConn, especially for current students who are working remotely or prospective student who are interested in applying.

process

Social Media Strategy

One of our team goals was to create documentation on our social strategy. When creating content, we wanted to make sure it was consistent in communication and tone, even when new team members will be working on social.

Audience and Content Guides

Our first step in documentation was to go back to our audiences and how our social could help them in their goals. Moving from here, we could consider what type of content would help each of our audiences them most and how often to post them. I was responsible for creating the final documentation based on feedack from my team.

Language Guide

To keep a consistent tone, I developed a language guide that described how we write for our audiences. It requires a kind, friendly, and supportive tone. I documented these principles as well as common words to use. Next steps would be to document specific phrases and sentences as well.

Social Media Calendar

At the end of every month, we begin planning the social media calendar for the following month. We gather events from organizations, find any major academic deadlines, and determine any holidays to celebrate. Through out the month, we might select specific days to highlight a student or post a campus photo.

Visual Design Guidelines

One of major projects at The Graduate School was to define brand and visual system for our social media. My process involved getting input from other members of the team, experimenting with different visual styles, and then getting feedback from the Communication Lead.

Our social strategy captures many different types of content, from academic reminders to fun celebration. For our visual design guidelines, we wanted to capture a wide range of tones and subject matters.

Typography

Varying categories of content, from formal academic reminders to fun posts about National Cookie Day, called for a wide range of typefaces. I decided to go with three different typefaces for social media, each one being used for a different mood.

Type Hierarchy

As these typefaces were used, I established a consistent hierarchy at which they could be used. These were helpful when creating content quickly and when designing social media templates.

Color

The same principle followed with color: sustaining a diverse set of colors. I decided to go with a lighly less saturated versions of each color to give a light softness and kindness to the brand.

Usage of Palette

With such a broad range of colors, there is the risk that too many could be used in a single composition. To get around this issue, every image post should use only 1-3 of the above colors, each of which can be split into tones and shades.

Shape

To align to the established principles of kindness and friendliness, all illustrations and graphics should employ rounded shapes and lines to give an organic nature to all images.
