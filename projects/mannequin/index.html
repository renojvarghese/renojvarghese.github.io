Projection Mapping on a Mannequin

Background

The idea of the project came from what could be possible with projection mapping technology. I wanted to prototype a new type of store display, in which mannequins could showcase multiple styles. The concept evolved to show what could be done by integrating technology into clothing. For example, consider how somone could change the color and style of their shirt by just swiping their fingers across their chest. You can find my prototypes below.

View Final Prototype

Process

The mapping process involved tracing over the project to find the shape of the mannequin and its features. Afterwards, I used After Effects to animate a video that showcased different styles of sunglasses. Below is an example of the mask and animation in After Effects.

Initial Prototype

The first test run, videoed below, is the result of projecting my animation on to a mannequin mannequin head. The animation changes the colors of hair, eyebrows, lipstick, and sunglasses to show the potential for accessory store displays.

Final Show

For the final show, I used a full-mannequin for the display. I took it as opportunity to see what can be done with technology embedded in clothing Clothing could be animated graphics, might biometric data, and switch styles within seconds. My projection was presented at my school's open house. Prospective students and their families came to see this performance.
